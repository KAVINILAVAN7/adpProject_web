Popular Toppings

Traditional: Mozzarella, tomatoes, basil, olive oil, mushrooms, pepperoni, sausage.

Gourmet/Creative: Prosciutto, arugula, truffles, figs, barbecue chicken, pineapple.

These are the more traditional ingredients typically associated with Italian pizza and popular in pizzerias worldwide. Cheese: Mozzarella is the most commonly used cheese on pizza, but other varieties like provolone, Parmesan, ricotta, and gorgonzola are also popular. Tomato Sauce: A key base for many pizzas, made from tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, and sometimes herbs. Pepperoni: A spicy, cured sausage that's extremely popular in the U.S. It adds a savory, slightly spicy flavor. Sausage: Italian sausage, which can be mild or spicy, is another classic meat topping. Mushrooms: Sliced mushrooms add an earthy, umami flavor and pair well with many other toppings. Onions: Raw or caramelized, onions add sweetness and a bit of crunch. Bell Peppers: Colorful and slightly sweet, they bring a crisp texture and fresh flavor. Black Olives: A slightly bitter and salty addition, often used in Mediterranean-style pizzas. Ham/Prosciutto: Ham adds a savory taste, while prosciutto offers a more delicate, salty flavor. Basil: Fresh basil leaves are a staple of the Margherita pizza, offering a fragrant and slightly sweet touch.


Neapolitan Pizza: The traditional pizza from Naples, Italy. It has a thin, soft crust, minimal toppings, and is cooked in a very hot wood-fired oven. The dough is typically made with just water, salt, yeast, and flour. New York-Style Pizza: Known for its large, foldable slices and crispy outer crust. The dough is stretched thin and is often sold by the slice. Chicago-Style Deep Dish: A thick, hearty pizza with a deep crust and layers of cheese, toppings, and chunky tomato sauce. It has a focaccia-like texture. Detroit-Style Pizza: Similar to Sicilian, but it is rectangular and cooked in a deep, square pan, which gives it a crispy, cheesy crust. It often has the sauce added on top of the cheese. Roman Pizza: Typically thinner and crispier than Neapolitan pizza. It can also be served "al taglio" (by the slice) and is popular as street food in Italy.
